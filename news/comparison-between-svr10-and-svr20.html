SVR10 - SVR20

Comparison of SVR10 and SVR20 Rubber for Businesses

SVR10 and SVR20 are two widely used grades of natural technical rubber in various industrial sectors today. Each type has its own characteristics in terms of purity, technical properties, cost, and application purpose.

Choosing the right raw material helps businesses optimize production costs, improve product quality, and meet technical standards across different industries.

SVR10 and SVR20 are commonly used in:

  • Tire manufacturing industry
  • Industrial conveyor belt production
  • Rubber gasket manufacturing
  • Mechanical engineering industry
  • General industrial rubber products

What is SVR10 Rubber?

SVR10 is a higher-grade natural rubber compared to SVR20, with fewer impurities and higher purity. It is commonly used in industries requiring high technical standards.

  • High purity
  • Low impurity content
  • Good durability and elasticity
  • Suitable for high-technical products

What is SVR20 Rubber?

SVR20 is a widely used natural rubber grade thanks to its reasonable cost, stable supply, and suitability for large-scale industrial production.

  • More cost-effective
  • Widely used in industrial applications
  • Suitable for mass production
  • Stable raw material supply

SVR10 vs SVR20 Comparison

Criteria SVR10 SVR20
Purity Higher Industrial standard
Impurities Lower Higher than SVR10
Price Higher More cost-efficient
Applications High-technical products General industrial production
Market demand Export & technical use Mass production

Modern Production Process

Today, SVR10 and SVR20 production lines are equipped with modern systems including soaking tanks, milling machines, crumb cutters, and advanced drying kilns, helping remove impurities and ensure stable product quality.

All raw materials are strictly inspected, processed, and controlled at every stage to meet technical standards for both domestic and export markets.

Should Businesses Choose SVR10 or SVR20?

The choice between SVR10 and SVR20 depends on production needs, technical requirements, and business budget.

  • If high quality and low impurities are required → choose SVR10
  • If cost optimization is the priority → SVR20 is suitable
  • For high-precision technical products → prefer SVR10
  • For large-scale production → SVR20 is more efficient

Conclusion

Both SVR10 and SVR20 are important natural rubber grades in the modern rubber industry. Each type serves different production needs, helping businesses choose the most suitable material based on their technical requirements and cost targets.
